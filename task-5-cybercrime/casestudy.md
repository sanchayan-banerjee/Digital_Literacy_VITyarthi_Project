# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud (Real Incident):

## What the Crime is:
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime in India where attackers trick users into authorizing payments. Several widely reported cases have involved multiple victims being targeted through fake payment requests on platforms like Google Pay and PhonePe.
## How the crime typically happens:
In a typical incident reported by The Times of India, scammers posed as buyers on online marketplaces. First, they contacted sellers and agreed to purchase items. Next, instead of sending money, they sent a “collect request” via UPI and instructed the seller to approve it to receive payment. Believing this, the victims entered their UPI PIN, which actually authorizes a debit transaction. In some cases, scammers also use QR codes or ask for OTPs to carry out the fraud.
## Who is usually targeted:
These scams commonly target students, small sellers and individuals unfamiliar with UPI or online banking systems.
## Consequences of the crime:
The consequences include immediate financial loss, mental stress and difficulty in recovering the stolen amount.
## Preventive Measures:
This type of fraud can be prevented by keeping in mind that entering a UPI PIN always authorizes payment, not receiving money. Users should never approve unknown transaction requests or share OTPs. Increasing awareness and verifying transactions carefully are essential steps in averting such cybercrimes.

Source: The Times of India, multiple reports on UPI fraud incidents in India (2023-2024).
